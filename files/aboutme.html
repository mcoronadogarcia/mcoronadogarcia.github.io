Mayra Coronado Garcia Biostatistician

I am a second year master's of public health (MPH) candidate in the department of biostatistic's. Before attending the University of Iowa College of Public Health, I attended Regis University. At Regis, I obtained a Bachelor's of Science in Mathematics with minor's in Spanish and Health Care Administration. As an undergraduate I was apart of two summer research programs where the projects I worked on focused on people who inject drugs. Currently, through my graduate research assistantship at the Prevention Research Center I have been working on the Active Ottumwa research project where I have been conducting statistical analysis and data management for the team. In the future, I hope to be working on research projects related to minority health disparities and drug abuse and use. Aside from academics and research, I mainly spend my time with my two year old puggle named Penny and my fiance, Michael. Most of the time, I am in the kitchen cooking a dish I found on pinterest or making a craft while re-watching episodes of Naruto.
