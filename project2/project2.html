Crude Death Rate of Opioid Overdoses in United States from 1999 to 2014

Discription of Map and Patterns: The choropleth maps below show crude death rates for opioid overdoses in the United states for 4 different years. These years are 1999, 2004, 2009, and 2014. Below each choropleth map is a histogram that displays the distribution of the crude death rates for the United States during each year. The reason crude death rates are being displayed is because it is the recommended way of standardizing deaths in a population. This allows us to compare states by looking at specific deaths for 1000 total deaths caused by a variety of reasons. Here our specific reason was deaths caused by opioid overdoses. In order to compare the year data, I would recommend viewing the data using the quantile breks and with 5 class levels. With those specifications, it is clear that the maps are showing that 20% of the crude death rates for opioid overdoses are above 140 per 1000 deaths for any reason since 1999 until 2014. Additionally, we see that since 2004, 20% of the crude death rates are between 120 and 140 opioid related deaths per 1000 deaths for any reason; However, the lower 40% of crude rates seem to have stayed within similar ranges and staying mostly spread out.

Note: If you hover over a state you would like to learn more about you will see a lengd appear with the states information on all four choropleth mapes.

Future Steps: Future steps include incorporating a zoom in feature to allow the data to be displayed larger for people who are interested in a specific region. Additionally, fixing the mouse over functionality that allows you to fade out regions you are not hovering over.
