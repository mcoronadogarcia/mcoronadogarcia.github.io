Choropleth Map and Scatterplot of Opioid Overdoses in US between 1999-2014

The maps you're looking at displays the crude death rate per 1000 deaths of Opioid Overdoses in the United States between 1999 and 2014. For example, in 1999 the crude death rate for Opioid Overdoses in Alaska was 108. This means that of 1000 deaths, 108 of them were due to Opioid Overdoeses. The choropleth displays information for the year selected. The scatterplot is showing that the there has been an increase in opioid overdoses overall as time continues. Although there is an overall increase, an intersting thing to note about this data iscfrom the scatter plot, is that there are some states who have stayed relatively the same, some who increased, and some who decreased opioid overdoses. The map was inspired by the somehwat-common population prymaids from various sources online. Unfortunately while the prymaid allows you a finer grained analysis, the form doesn't lend itself to integrating more than one country at a time.

  • There are three primary points of interaction; brushing scatterplot axes, the dropdown attribute selection, and the map hovering. Each of them aid in assisting the user to get the data they need by zooming and filtering the data.
  • The map tooltip interaction allows precise information, thus serving as a proxy for details on demand.

Future Modifications All the features for each region is being displayed in the scatterplot including variables that are not relevant. Furture work includes only displaying the years on this plot.
